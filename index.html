About Me

I am a biochemist and structural biologist with extensive experience in protein engineering and molecular biology, specifically focusing on photoreceptor proteins and their mechanisms of light-induced signal transduction.

My doctoral research at Stony Brook University centered on probing the signal transduction mechanism of the blue light-activated adenylate cyclase OaPAC using unnatural amino acid mutagenesis. This work has contributed to understanding how ultrafast light-induced changes in protein structure lead to enzyme activation.

I am passionate about leveraging innovative molecular techniques to gain mechanistic insights into complex biological systems, with particular expertise in protein purification, characterization, and structural analysis using techniques such as X-ray crystallography and various spectroscopic methods.

Research Areas

Protein Engineering

Utilizing unnatural amino acid mutagenesis to engineer proteins with modified properties and to understand structure-function relationships in photoreceptor proteins.

Photochemistry & Signal Transduction

Investigating how blue light activation triggers structural changes in BLUF photoreceptors and how these changes propagate to activate downstream enzymatic functions.

Structural Biology

Employing X-ray crystallography, spectroscopy, and biophysical techniques to determine protein structures and understand conformational dynamics at atomic resolution.
